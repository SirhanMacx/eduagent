## Answer Key

*(For teacher use only — do not distribute to students)*

**1.** sunlight (light energy), water, and carbon dioxide

**2.** *photo* = **light**; *synthesis* = **to put together**

**3.** An autotroph is an organism that makes its own food (e.g., a tree, grass, algae). A heterotroph is an organism that must eat other organisms for energy (e.g., a human, a dog, a mushroom). *(Accept any correct examples.)*

**4.** autotrophs; they make their own food through photosynthesis

**5.** *(Accept any two of the following:)*
- Photosynthesis produces oxygen, which is the gas that humans and other animals need to breathe.
- Photosynthesis is the base of nearly all food chains — without it, animals would have no food source.
- Photosynthesis removes carbon dioxide from the atmosphere, which helps regulate Earth's climate.

**6.**
- Label A (top): sunlight
- Label B (H₂O): water
- Label C (CO₂): carbon dioxide
- Label D (O₂): oxygen
- Label E (?): **glucose**

**7.** No, this is not correct. Plants get their energy from sunlight, not from soil. While plants absorb water and minerals from the soil through their roots, the energy to make food (glucose) comes from sunlight through the process of photosynthesis.

**8.** Sun (sunlight); plant (or autotroph/producer)

**9.** *(Accept any two reasonable responses, such as:)*
- Animals would run out of food and eventually die because plants are at the base of food chains.
- Oxygen levels in the atmosphere would drop because plants produce oxygen through photosynthesis.
- Carbon dioxide levels would rise because plants would no longer be absorbing CO₂.
- The climate would change dramatically because of the increase in CO₂ (greenhouse gas).

**10.** **D) Glucose**
