# Life From Light: Understanding Photosynthesis

**Subject:** Science
**Grade Level:** 8
**Topic:** Photosynthesis
**Duration:** 3 weeks (15 lessons)
**Teacher:** Ms. Johnson | Direct Instruction | Warm & Encouraging Tone

---

## Overview

In this three-week unit, students will explore how plants convert light energy into chemical energy through photosynthesis. Beginning with the big picture of energy flow in ecosystems, students will progress through the inputs, outputs, and internal mechanisms of the photosynthetic process. By the end of the unit, students will understand the chemical equation for photosynthesis, the roles of chloroplasts and chlorophyll, the light-dependent and light-independent reactions, and the critical importance of photosynthesis to all life on Earth. Activities emphasize hands-on observation, graphic organizers, collaborative discussion, and real-world connections.

---

## Essential Questions

1. How do plants make their own food, and why does it matter to every living thing on the planet?
2. What role does sunlight play in the chemical process of photosynthesis?
3. How are the inputs and outputs of photosynthesis connected to the cycling of matter in ecosystems?
4. What would happen to life on Earth if photosynthesis stopped?
5. How do the light-dependent and light-independent reactions work together to produce glucose?

---

## Enduring Understandings

1. Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert light energy, carbon dioxide, and water into glucose and oxygen — forming the foundation of nearly all food webs on Earth.
2. Energy flows through ecosystems because of photosynthesis; the Sun is the ultimate source of energy for most life.
3. The chemical equation for photosynthesis (6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂) represents a transformation of matter and energy that obeys the law of conservation of mass.

## Daily Lesson Sequence

| # | Topic | Type | Description |
|---|-------|------|-------------|
| 1 | What is Photosynthesis? The Big Picture | instruction | Introduce photosynthesis as the process plants use to make food from sunlight. Students explore why this matters to all living things. Graphic organizer comparing autotrophs and heterotrophs. |
| 2 | Energy From the Sun | instruction | Explore how sunlight is a form of energy and how it travels to Earth. Introduce the electromagnetic spectrum with a focus on visible light. |
| 3 | Inside the Leaf: Chloroplasts and Chlorophyll | instruction | Examine leaf structure using diagrams. Introduce chloroplasts as the "solar panels" of the cell and chlorophyll as the pigment that captures light. |
| 4 | Leaf Observation Lab | lab | Hands-on microscope lab: students observe prepared slides of leaf cross-sections, sketch chloroplasts, and record observations. |
| 5 | The Inputs: What Do Plants Need? | instruction | Deep dive into the raw materials of photosynthesis — carbon dioxide and water. Trace how each enters the plant (stomata, roots/xylem). |
| 6 | The Outputs: What Do Plants Make? | instruction | Explore glucose as stored chemical energy and oxygen as a byproduct. Connect to cellular respiration preview. Exit ticket: input/output sorting activity. |
| 7 | The Chemical Equation | instruction | Build the balanced chemical equation step by step. Students practice balancing and interpreting 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂. |
| 8 | Formative Check: Photosynthesis Basics | assessment | Mid-unit formative quiz covering lessons 1–7. Mix of multiple choice, fill-in-the-blank, and one short constructed response. |
| 9 | Light-Dependent Reactions | instruction | Introduce the light reactions in the thylakoid membrane. Simplified model: light energy splits water, produces ATP and NADPH, releases oxygen. |
| 10 | Light-Independent Reactions (Calvin Cycle) | instruction | Introduce the Calvin Cycle in the stroma. Simplified model: CO₂ is fixed into glucose using ATP and NADPH from the light reactions. |
| 11 | Putting It All Together: Two-Stage Model | instruction | Students build a two-column graphic organizer connecting light-dependent and light-independent reactions. Class discussion and gallery walk. |
| 12 | Factors That Affect Photosynthesis | instruction | Explore how light intensity, CO₂ concentration, temperature, and water availability affect the rate of photosynthesis. Graphing activity. |
| 13 | Elodea Lab: Measuring Photosynthesis | lab | Students count oxygen bubbles produced by Elodea under different light conditions to measure photosynthesis rate. Data collection and graphing. |
| 14 | Photosynthesis and the Global Carbon Cycle | instruction | Connect photosynthesis to the big picture: carbon cycling, climate, deforestation, and why plants matter for the planet. Class discussion and short video clip. |
| 15 | Summative Assessment: Photosynthesis Unit Test | assessment | End-of-unit test covering all major concepts: the equation, inputs/outputs, chloroplast structure, light and dark reactions, factors affecting rate, and ecological importance. Includes a diagram labeling section and one extended response. |

## Assessment Plan

### Formative Assessments

- **Daily Exit Tickets** (Lessons 1–7, 9–12, 14): 3-question quick checks at the end of each lesson to gauge understanding before moving on.
- **Leaf Observation Lab Report** (Lesson 4): Students submit labeled sketches and written observations from microscope work.
- **Mid-Unit Quiz** (Lesson 8): 15-point quiz covering the basics of photosynthesis (inputs, outputs, equation, chloroplast structure).
- **Two-Stage Graphic Organizer** (Lesson 11): Collected and scored using a rubric — must correctly connect light-dependent and light-independent reactions.
- **Elodea Lab Data Sheet** (Lesson 13): Students submit completed data tables and graphs with a short analysis paragraph.

### Summative Assessment

- **Photosynthesis Unit Test** (Lesson 15): 50-point comprehensive assessment including:
  - 20 multiple choice questions (1 pt each)
  - 5 fill-in-the-blank (2 pts each)
  - 2 diagram labeling sections (5 pts each)
  - 1 extended response: "Explain how photosynthesis supports life on Earth and what would happen if it stopped." (10 pts, rubric-scored)
