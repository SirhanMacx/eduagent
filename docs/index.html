The Causes of World War II: From Appeasement to Invasion

Grade 10 · US History · 55 minutes · CCSS.RH.9-10.2, CCSS.RH.9-10.6
Do Now (5 min)

Examine the political cartoon (Neville Chamberlain, "Peace for our time," 1938). In 2–3 sentences: What is the cartoonist arguing? What evidence in the image supports your claim?

Guided Notes
  • Treaty of Versailles — economic conditions that fueled resentment
  • Rise of totalitarianism: compare Hitler, Mussolini, and Tojo
  • Appeasement policy — Munich Agreement (1938), why Britain and France chose it
  • Invasion of Poland (September 1, 1939) — the breaking point
  • US neutrality vs. Lend-Lease — shifting American opinion
  • Pearl Harbor — from isolation to total war
Exit Ticket

1. Identify TWO causes of WWII and explain how they are connected. 2. Was appeasement a reasonable strategy in 1938? Defend your position with evidence. 3. How did American policy shift between 1939 and 1941?

Includes primary source images, slide deck, and printable student handout — all in one generation.

Get Started

Up and running in three commands

Works on Mac, Windows, and Linux. Python 3.10+ required.

# Install $ pip install clawed # Run setup (picks your AI model, creates workspace) $ clawed setup # Generate your first lesson $ clawed lesson "Your Topic Here"

The software is free. You bring your own AI provider — we recommend Ollama Cloud ($20/month unlimited) or use free-tier options to try it out. No account with us required.

When you run clawed ingest, it reads your lesson plans, worksheets, and assessments.
