# EDUagent ProductHunt Hunter Outreach

Having a well-known hunter submit your product gets you more visibility. Reach out 2-3 weeks before launch.

---

## Target Hunters

### 1. Chris Messina (@chrismessina)
- **Why:** Prolific hunter (1,000+ hunts), inventor of the hashtag, consistently hunts developer tools and AI products. Has hunted education-adjacent tools before. Massive following means built-in visibility.
- **Approach:** Twitter DM or email. Keep it short. He gets hundreds of requests.

### 2. Ben Tossell (@bentossell)
- **Why:** Founder of Makerpad (acquired by Zapier), deeply interested in no-code/low-code and tools that empower non-technical people. A teacher building an AI tool fits his ethos perfectly. Active hunter with strong EdTech sympathies.
- **Approach:** Twitter DM. Reference the "non-developer building tools" angle.

### 3. Akshay Kothari (@akothari)
- **Why:** COO of Notion, frequent hunter, passionate about productivity and knowledge tools. EDUagent's "learn from your documents" approach overlaps with Notion's philosophy. Has hunted AI writing tools.
- **Approach:** LinkedIn or Twitter. Frame it as a knowledge management tool for educators.

### 4. Mushtaq Bilal (@MushtaqBilalPhD)
- **Why:** PhD academic and educator with massive Twitter/X following in the AI-for-education space. Regularly posts about AI tools for teaching and research. Not a traditional PH hunter, but has the audience and credibility — and would hunt it for the story alone.
- **Approach:** Twitter DM. Lead with the "I'm a teacher who built this" story. He amplifies educator-built tools.

### 5. Kevin William David (@kevinwdavid)
- **Why:** One of ProductHunt's most active hunters with 1,500+ hunts. Hunts broadly across AI, developer tools, and productivity. Reliable and responsive. Less selective than top-tier hunters, which means higher chance of a yes.
- **Approach:** Twitter DM or ProductHunt DM. Send a clear one-liner and a link.

## Outreach Priority

1. **First choice:** Chris Messina or Ben Tossell (biggest reach)
2. **Backup:** Mushtaq Bilal (strongest EdTech alignment)
3. **Reliable fallback:** Kevin William David (most likely to say yes)

Reach out to 2-3 at a time. If your first choice says yes, politely thank the others and let them know. If nobody responds within a week, follow up once, then move to the next name.

## Template DM

Personalize this for each hunter. Do not send it as a mass copy-paste — they can tell.

---

**Subject (if email):** Quick question — would you hunt an open-source AI teaching tool?

**Message:**

Hey [Name],

I'm Jon, a middle school social studies teacher in New York. I've been teaching for 9 years and I built an open-source tool called EDUagent that I think you'd find interesting.

It reads a teacher's existing lesson plans and learns their teaching voice — structure, vocabulary, tone, everything. Then it generates new lessons, worksheets, and assessments that sound exactly like that teacher wrote them. Not generic AI output — output that sounds like *you* on a good day.

It runs locally, it's free, and it's MIT licensed. I use it for my own classes every week.

I'm planning to launch on ProductHunt on [DATE] and was wondering if you'd be interested in hunting it. I have all the assets ready (screenshots, description, maker comment) and I'll be online all day to respond to comments.

Here's the GitHub if you want to take a look: https://github.com/SirhanMacx/eduagent

Either way, appreciate your time. Thanks for all the great products you've surfaced.

— Jon

## Follow-Up (send 5-7 days after initial message if no response)

Hey [Name], just bumping this in case it got buried. Totally understand if it's not a fit — no pressure at all. The launch is [DATE] if you're interested. Either way, cheers.

## If No Hunter Says Yes

Self-hunting is completely fine. Many successful products self-hunt. If you go this route:
- Make sure your ProductHunt profile is complete (photo, bio, Twitter linked)
- Your maker comment becomes even more important (see `maker-comment.md`)
- Focus extra energy on the community launch plan (see `communities.md`)
