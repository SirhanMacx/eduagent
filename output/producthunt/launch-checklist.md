# EDUagent ProductHunt Launch Checklist

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Launch (2-3 weeks before)

### Submit to "Coming Soon"
- [ ] Go to [producthunt.com/posts/new](https://www.producthunt.com/posts/new)
- [ ] Select "Coming Soon" page
- [ ] Upload logo (240x240px, PNG, no text — use the graduation cap icon)
- [ ] Set tagline (pick one from Section 5 below)
- [ ] Set description (use the 260-char version from Section 6 below)
- [ ] Add topics: **Education**, **Artificial Intelligence**, **Productivity**, **Developer Tools**
- [ ] Add links: GitHub repo, website, Twitter/X
- [ ] Publish the Coming Soon page

### Build Supporter Base (target: 20+ subscribers)
- [ ] Share Coming Soon link with teacher friends and colleagues at Great Neck South
- [ ] Post Coming Soon link in personal Twitter/X bio
- [ ] Share in r/Teachers with a genuine "built this for myself" post
- [ ] Share in EdTech Slack/Discord communities (see `communities.md`)
- [ ] Email 10-15 teacher contacts personally — ask them to subscribe
- [ ] Post in any alumni groups or teacher networks you belong to
- [ ] Goal: **20 subscribers minimum** before scheduling launch

### Schedule Launch
- [ ] Schedule for a **Tuesday** at **12:01 AM PST** (3:01 AM EST)
  - Tuesday-Thursday are highest-traffic days on ProductHunt
  - 12:01 AM PST means you get the full 24-hour voting window
- [ ] Confirm launch date at least 1 week in advance
- [ ] Set a calendar reminder for 11:50 PM PST the night before

## Phase 3: Listing Copy

### Tagline Options (60 characters max)

Pick one. All tested at 60 chars or fewer:

1. `AI co-teacher that writes lessons in your voice` (50 chars)
2. `Your lesson plans train an AI that teaches like you` (52 chars)
3. `Open-source AI that learns your teaching style` (47 chars)
4. `Feed it your lessons. It writes new ones like you.` (51 chars)
5. `AI lesson generator trained on your own materials` (50 chars)

**Recommended:** Option 1 or 4. Option 1 is clearest. Option 4 is punchiest.

### Description (260 characters for ProductHunt)

```
EDUagent reads your existing lesson plans and learns your teaching fingerprint — style, structure, vocabulary, tone. Then it generates new lessons, worksheets, and assessments that sound exactly like you wrote them. Open-source. Runs locally. Your files stay yours.
```

(261 chars — trim "exactly " if needed to hit 260)

### Topics
Select these four when submitting:
- Education
- Artificial Intelligence
- Productivity
- Developer Tools

## Phase 4: Launch Day

### T-minus 30 minutes (11:30 PM PST night before)
- [ ] Draft your maker comment (see `maker-comment.md`)
- [ ] Have community post links ready (see `communities.md`)
- [ ] Open ProductHunt dashboard in browser
- [ ] Text/DM your hunter to confirm they're ready

### T-zero (12:01 AM PST)
- [ ] Confirm listing is live
- [ ] Post maker comment immediately (see `maker-comment.md`)
- [ ] Tweet/X post with ProductHunt link
- [ ] Begin community posting schedule (see `communities.md`)

### First 2 hours (12:00-2:00 AM PST)
- [ ] Respond to every comment on ProductHunt within 15 minutes
- [ ] Monitor for questions — be genuine, not salesy
- [ ] DM 10 close contacts asking them to check it out (not "upvote" — that violates PH rules)

### Morning push (6:00-8:00 AM PST / 9:00-11:00 AM EST)
- [ ] Second round of community posts (see timing in `communities.md`)
- [ ] Email your subscriber list
- [ ] Share in teacher group chats

### Throughout the day
- [ ] Reply to every single ProductHunt comment — this is critical
- [ ] Share interesting comments/questions on Twitter/X
- [ ] Thank supporters publicly
- [ ] Post a progress update comment at 12:00 PM PST

### End of day (9:00 PM PST)
- [ ] Post a thank-you comment on ProductHunt regardless of placement
- [ ] Tweet a summary of the day
- [ ] Note what worked for next time

---

## Phase 5: Post-Launch (week after)

- [ ] Write a "lessons learned" post for your blog or Twitter/X
- [ ] Follow up with everyone who commented or reached out
- [ ] Add "Featured on ProductHunt" badge to README if applicable
- [ ] Share final results with communities that supported the launch
- [ ] Channel new GitHub stars into contributor pipeline (good first issues)
