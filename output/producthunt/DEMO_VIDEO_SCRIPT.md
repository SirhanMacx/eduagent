# EDUagent Demo Video Script (60 seconds)

**Format:** Teacher records on phone, screen recording with voiceover.
**Vibe:** Casual, like showing a colleague something cool in the teachers' lounge.

---

## 0–8 seconds: The Problem

**[Show: Empty lesson plan template on screen]**

**Say:** "I've been teaching social studies for nine years. Every Sunday night — three hours minimum planning lessons from scratch. And every AI tool I tried? Sounded nothing like me."

---

## 8–18 seconds: The Ingest

**[Show: Terminal. Type the command live.]**

```bash
eduagent ingest ~/Documents/Teaching/
```

**Say:** "EDUagent reads your actual lesson plans. PDFs, Word docs, PowerPoints — whatever you've got."

**[Show: The progress bar running, then the persona summary printing out]**

**Say:** "It pulls out your teaching fingerprint. My style, my AIM questions, my Do Nows."

---

## 18–35 seconds: Generate a Unit

**[Show: Terminal. Type the command live.]**

```bash
eduagent unit "Causes of World War I" --grade 10 --weeks 2
```

**Say:** "Now ask it to plan a unit. Two weeks on the causes of World War I for my tenth graders."

**[Show: Unit plan streaming out — essential questions, lesson sequence, pacing calendar]**

**Say:** "Essential questions, ten daily lessons, pacing — and it actually sounds like something I'd hand out."

---

## 35–48 seconds: The Lesson Close-Up

**[Show: Scroll to a single lesson in the output]**

**Say:** "Look at this lesson. AIM question: 'How did the alliance system turn a regional conflict into a world war?' That's my format. Do Now with a primary source. Document analysis. Exit ticket. That's how I teach."

---

## 48–55 seconds: The Kicker

**[Show: Terminal, fresh prompt]**

**Say:** "It also makes worksheets, assessments, sub packets, IEP accommodations — all in your voice."

---

## 55–60 seconds: The CTA

**[Show: Terminal with the install command]**

```bash
pip install eduagent
```

**Say:** "Open source. Free forever. Link in the description."

**[End card: eduagent on GitHub — star us if it saves you a Sunday night]**

---

## Recording Tips

- Record screen with QuickTime or OBS, voiceover on phone mic
- Keep terminal font big (16pt+) — viewers will watch on phones
- Don't rush the typing — let people read the commands
- The WWI unit output is the money shot — let it stream for 3-4 seconds
- Smile in the thumbnail if you include a face cam
